Adolf Hitler

Here's a timeline with significant events related to Adolf Hitler:

  • 1889: Adolf Hitler is born on April 20th in Braunau am Inn, Austria-Hungary.
  • 1907-1913: Hitler moves to Vienna, experiences poverty, and develops his political and anti-Semitic views.
  • 1914-1918: Serves in World War I, awarded the Iron Cross for bravery, and experiences the German defeat, fostering resentment towards the Treaty of Versailles.
  • 1919: Joins the German Workers' Party, which later becomes the National Socialist German Workers' Party (Nazi Party).
  • 1923: Hitler's failed Beer Hall Putsch in Munich results in his brief imprisonment, during which he writes "Mein Kampf."
  • 1933: Hitler becomes Chancellor of Germany and begins consolidating power, suppressing opposition, and implementing anti-Semitic laws.
  • 1938: Anschluss - Germany annexes Austria, and the Munich Agreement leads to the annexation of the Sudetenland from Czechoslovakia.
  • 1939: Germany invades Poland, triggering the start of World War II. The Holocaust begins with mass extermination campaigns.
  • 1941: Operation Barbarossa - Germany invades the Soviet Union.
  • 1942: Wannsee Conference - High-ranking officials coordinate the "Final Solution," intensifying the genocide.
  • 1945: As Allied forces advance, Hitler commits suicide in his bunker in Berlin on April 30th. Germany surrenders on May 7th, marking the end of World War II in Europe.
